Color Theory

Color theory is the study of how colors work together and how they affect our emotions and perceptions.

Project
Color theory is the study of colors and their interactions. It serves as a foundation for creating visually appealing and effective designs. The fundamental elements of color theory include the color wheel, color harmony, and the psychological impact of colors. By understanding these components, designers can make informed choices about color use in various contexts.
The color wheel is a circular diagram that organizes colors based on their relationships. At its core are the primary colors—red, blue, and yellow—which are the building blocks for all other colors. Secondary colors, such as green, orange, and purple, are created by mixing two primary colors. Tertiary colors result from mixing a primary color with a secondary color, producing hues like red-orange or blue-green.
Color Psychology: Different colors can evoke different emotions and reactions. For example, blue is often associated with calm and trust, while red can evoke excitement and urgency.
